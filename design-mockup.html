Palace Audio Mixing Engineer

how does my mix sound right now?
Integrated LUFS is −14.2 with 1.3 dB true-peak headroom. The 80 Hz region is hot — roughly 4 dB above a balanced low end. Crest factor of 9.8 dB says this is still a WIP. Tighten the sub before mastering.
is the kick fighting the bass?
No. KICK and SUBS show anti-correlated energy in the 60–120 Hz range — that's sidechain ducking working, not masking. The two coexist cleanly.
what's making the high end harsh?
Two suspects. HATS has a strong 6.8 kHz peak — about 5 dB above where most genres want it. CLAP has a saturation device with dry/wet at 92% adding 3rd-order harmonics around 4–9 kHz. The clap is probably the bigger contributor.
Decisions baked in across all four:
  • Gear icon removed. Settings (API key entry, change, clear) already live inside the chat panel — adding a second gear on the strip was redundant. Freed up space everywhere.
  • GitHub icon moved next to the brand name in three of the four (corner-tucked in B). One small button instead of a dedicated icon row at the bottom.
  • The four typography variations: A "Mixing Engineer" semibold sans · B "MIXING ENGINEER" all-caps small letter-spaced · C "MIX·ENG" mono abbreviation · D "mixing engineer" lowercase italic. Each shifts the brand mood — confident product / museum-label / studio-gear / calm-independent.
  • Logo treatment differs by design: A small mid-strip slot · B large brand visual at top · C quiet footer stamp · D middle floater between metrics and chat.
  • Chat button position: A/B/C let it sit naturally below their content blocks; D pins it at the very bottom with the logo absorbing the breathing room above. The strip dimensions (140 × 169) stay constant.
